Mining Egocentric Temporal Motifs

Temporal graphs are structures which model relational data between entities that change over time. Due to the complex structure of data, mining statistically significant temporal subgraphs, also known as temporal motifs, is a challenging task. In this work, we present an efficient technique for extracting temporal motifs in temporal networks. Our method is based on the novel notion of egocentric temporal neighborhoods, namely multi-layer structures centered on an ego node. Each temporal layer of the structure consists of the first-order neighborhood of the ego node, and corresponding nodes in sequential layers are connected by an edge. The strength of this approach lies in the possibility of encoding these structures into a unique bit vector, thus bypassing the problem of graph isomorphism in searching for temporal motifs. This allows our algorithm to mine substantially larger motifs with respect to alternative approaches. Furthermore, by bringing the focus on the temporal dynamics of the interactions of a specific node, our model allows to mine temporal motifs which are visibly interpretable. Experiments on a number of complex networks of social interactions confirm the advantage of the proposed approach over alternative non-egocentric solutions. The egocentric procedure is indeed more efficient in revealing similarities and discrepancies among different social environments, independently of the different technologies used to collect data, which instead affect standard non-egocentric measures.

Graphical summary of the procedure for extracting egocentric temporal motifs. The left panel shows the egocentric temporal neighborhood of the ego node E (in green), with temporal order two and initial time instant zero. Black edges connect the central node with its neighbors (in red) at each time step, while green (resp. red) edges connect consecutive occurrences of the central (resp. a neighboring) node along the time sequence. The right panel shows how the corresponding egocentric temporal neighborhood signature (ETNS) is computed. Each neighboring node is encoded into a bit vector indicating the time slots when it is present. The node encodings are lexicographically sorted first and then concatenated to generate the signature.

Temporal Network Generation

Generating temporal network is an essential task to preserve privacy or to increase the size of the input network in both the temporal dimension and in terms of nodes. Many algorithms have been propose to generate synthetic static networks. On the other hand, some techniques have been proposed in the last years to generate synthetic temporal networks. However, the proposed techniques sometimes fail in correctly reproducing the local pattern of interactions of the singular involved nodes and the temporal correlations. Moreover, the proposed techniques have not been developed to handle small temporal resolutions. Finally, they are not always able to reproduce the temporal periodicity of interaction that characterize some types of temporal networks such as face-to-face interactions, SMS and calls. Here, we propose a novel temporal graph generative model, based on the notion of Egocentric Temporal Neighbourhood. In this paper, we show the effectiveness of the egocentric perspective in the generative process of temporal network. Moreover, we empirically evaluate the generated networks both in terms of temporal structure and of behavior of dynamical systems.
